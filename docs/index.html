Patient Experience Education & Development

Empathy in Action, Education in Focus

Our Compassionate Journey Through Heartfelt Learning

Over the past year, education has taken center stage. The Office of Patient and Customer Experience (OPCE) has strategically employed a mix of learning modalities to address the diverse training and developmental needs of our team members. Implementing various approaches has been crucial in meeting the increased demand for customized site-based sessions. While staying true to fundamental concepts, OPCE consistently evolves the Culture of C.A.R.E., our commitment to patients and families, ensuring that the curriculum remains relevant and adaptable. The expanded range of course offerings has emphasized the significance of Culture of C.A.R.E. train-the-trainer workshops, educating over 100 prospective champions on the updated content and facilitation skills.

A noteworthy educational initiative includes the ongoing Relationship-Centered Communication (RCC) course. Rooted in the Culture of C.A.R.E., RCC is an immersive learning program with minimal didactics, placing a primary focus on experiential learning in communication skills through role play, real-time mentoring, and coaching. It goes beyond purely lessons; it embodies a language deeply grounded in evidence-based communication, seamlessly bridging the gap between patients and providers, essential for authentic empathy. The success of the program has gained recognition from the Journal of Patient Experience, which published a narrative titled “From Monologue to Dialogue: The Pursuit of Relationship-Centered Communication Across a Large, Integrated Healthcare System.”

Business Intelligence

Driving Continuous Process Improvement

Leveraging data-driven insights to effectively target and prioritize continuous process improvement efforts through the lens of patient experience

It is important that business and work unit leaders throughout the organization have the necessary tools and support for continuously identifying and prioritizing areas for improvement. Therefore, the OPCE Business Intelligence team has partnered with regional ambulatory culture leaders and operational leadership on the deployment of a continuous process improvement model designed to proactively identify practice locations with patient experience improvement needs. Leveraging our regional support model, the team engages each region on a quarterly cadence with a list of locations with the greatest opportunity for improvement. The analysis is based on quantitative and qualitative feedback that is trended and benchmarked both internally and externally. Locations that are deemed ‘change ready’ by regional leadership are then activated onto a Plan, Do, Study, Act (PDSA) improvement cycle with specific improvement goals. As local practice management leads the improvement effort with support from regional operations leadership, the OPCE BI team delivers ongoing analytics, best practice, knowledge management, and progress tracking support. Every quarter, active PDSA locations are evaluated for a disposition of ‘Graduate’, ‘Extend’, or ‘Discontinue’ while new locations are activated to offset the graduated and discontinued efforts.

Getting dreamy

Modernizing our business intelligence capabilities by moving to the cloud

As a business intelligence organization, we have access to a lot of data. In addition to the quantitative and qualitative insights we gather in the roughly 500,000 Patient Experience surveys we get each year, we also have access to a wealth of data captured from the patient’s clinical encounter. This is all used to enhance our dashboards with more explanatory data and allows us to produce more in-depth analyses for the organization. However, that depth of and breadth of data comes at a cost, and we’ve realized that we’ve hit the physical limitations of our traditional business intelligence environment. To truly push the envelope of services we provide we need to take advantage of a modern data platform that will grow with us as we grow our team. We have partnered with the Northwell Health Cloud Center of Excellence to migrate our data architecture to the cloud. This will enable us to continue to enhance the services we provide while the organization grows around us, as well as offer new capabilities that we had previously been unable to provide due to resource constraints. This includes deeper insights to the patient experience, increased self-service capabilities, and customizable on-demand reporting beyond the Tableau dashboards.

Partnership

A renewed partnership in the spirit of innovation and transformation

Northwell Health and Press Ganey have renewed their partnership with the goal of revolutionizing how the human experience in healthcare is gathered, understood, and acted upon by leveraging advanced technologies such as artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning (ML), and natural language processing (NLP). Together, we are also introducing innovative approaches like the Human Effort Score (HES) and patient experience profiling to gain deeper insights into individual patient preferences and needs.

Through AI and ML algorithms, Northwell analyzes vast amounts of healthcare data to predict patient experience outcomes and personalize interactions, while NLP technology extracts valuable insights from unstructured data sources such as patient feedback.

The introduction of the HES will allow Northwell to quantify the level of effort patients perceive when interacting with healthcare services, identifying areas of digital and non-digital points of friction and implementing targeted interventions to streamline processes and reduce patient burden. Additionally, patient experience profiling techniques will enable Northwell to create comprehensive profiles for each patient, incorporating demographic information, medical history, and communication preferences.

By analyzing past interactions and feedback, Northwell anticipates patient preferences, proactively addresses concerns, and allocates resources effectively, leading to improved health outcomes and stronger connections between patients and providers. This collaborative effort between Northwell Health and Press Ganey signifies a commitment to patient-centered care and continuous innovation in the healthcare industry.

Elevating Taste and Wellness:

Where Culinary Brilliance Meets Clinical Expertise

The goal of the food and nutrition department is to elevate how healthcare professionals view food, recognizing there is an intrinsic responsibility and obligation to lead by example, prioritizing food and nutrition as an essential facet of health and care delivery. Northwell has spearheaded this space as recognized by numerous spotlights on national television, podcasts, national presentations, research journals, and other prestigious publications such as PEOPLE magazine, Washington Post, Wall Street Journal and Becker’s Healthcare Review. The quality of the food reached its all-time high in 2023, consistently ranking in the 84 percentile. Northwell signed partnership agreements with The Center for Discovery in Monticello, NY, along with local Queens County Farm to procure fresh produce, and to open education throughout the community on what it means to “eat healthy.”

Nutritional Achievements

Registered dietitian/nutritionist leadership spearheaded the creation of a Standardized Adult Nutrition Formulary for enteral and oral nutrition. 93% of sites added plant-based oral/enteral products and 64% of sites reported >25% increase in plant-based products used. Another highlight was the addition of a standardized nutrition screen malnutrition screening tool (MST) to Nursing risk screens. This enabled timely nutrition care to patients at nutritional risk potentially reducing length of stay and readmission rates. Malnutrition toolkits for team education were enhanced and system policies were updated. Looking ahead, we continue to grow and develop CSA partnerships for local, fresh produce in our hospitals.

An experiential learning program

Ambulatory Administrative Academy

By bringing together cross-functional teams from across the enterprise, the team developed the first-ever in-person, structured training for ambulatory administrative team members – the Ambulatory Administrative Academy (AAA). Covering an enhanced Culture of C.A.R.E. with an emphasis on patient access and patient accommodation, the AAA aims to set behavioral and professional expectations by impressing Northwell values, empower new team members with baseline tools and education to be “ready to work”, and build a standard approach to key functions in ambulatory practices.

The AAA launched in a phased approach – first by engaging, celebrating, and empowering our Practice Leadership. These in-person mandatory half-day sessions explore best practices that can address the evolving landscape of healthcare management and provide tools and strategies to support access and elevate patient care and experience. Since the launch in February 2024, close to 500 practice leaders completed the workshop. The second phase of the AAA will focus on the newly hired ambulatory administrative team members. During the two-week training, the AAA will cover enhanced Culture of C.A.R.E. with an emphasis on patient access and patient accommodation, hands-on scheduling technology training and best practices, telephony operations and etiquette, revenue cycle practices, digital front door tools, and become the future home to new digital technology training such as Salesforce.
